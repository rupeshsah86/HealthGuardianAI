Emergency Guides

Step-by-step instructions for the most critical emergencies.

1

Call emergency services (102) immediately — do not wait.

2

Have the person sit or lie down in a comfortable position.

3

Loosen tight clothing around the neck and chest.

4

If prescribed, help them take nitroglycerin.

5

Give aspirin (325mg) to chew if not allergic.

6

Begin CPR if the person becomes unresponsive and stops breathing.

F

Face — Ask to smile. Does one side droop?

A

Arms — Ask to raise both arms. Does one drift down?

S

Speech — Ask to repeat a phrase. Is speech slurred or strange?

T

Time — If ANY of these, call 102 immediately. Note the time symptoms started.

!

Do NOT give food, water, or medication. Keep them calm and still.

1

Ask "Are you choking?" — if they cannot speak or cough, act immediately.

2

Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.

3

Make a fist and place it just above the navel, below the breastbone.

4

Grasp your fist with the other hand and give quick upward thrusts.

5

Repeat until the object is expelled or the person loses consciousness.

6

If unconscious, begin CPR and call 102.

1

Call emergency services immediately.

2

Wear gloves if available. Remove clothing to expose the wound.

3

Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.

4

If blood soaks through, add more cloth on top — do NOT remove the first layer.

5

Elevate the injured area above heart level if possible.

6

Keep the person warm and calm until help arrives.

1

Identify and remove the allergen if possible (food, insect, medication).

2

For mild reactions: take an antihistamine (e.g. cetirizine or diphenhydramine).

3

Apply cool compress to itchy or swollen skin areas.

4

For severe reaction (anaphylaxis): use epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if available.

5

Call 102 immediately if breathing difficulty, throat swelling, or loss of consciousness occurs.

6

Lay the person flat with legs elevated unless breathing is difficult — then sit them upright.

1

Remove the person from the source of heat immediately.

2

Cool the burn with cool (not cold) running water for 10–20 minutes.

3

Remove jewelry or tight items near the burn before swelling starts.

4

Do NOT apply butter, toothpaste, or ice — these worsen burns.

5

Cover loosely with a sterile, non-stick dressing or clean cloth.

6

Seek medical help for burns larger than 3 inches or on face/hands/joints.
